Privacy Policy — Big Grass Guy

Power of Grass · Last updated 24 June 2026

Big Grass Guy (“the app,” “we,” “us”) is a lawn-care app published by Power of Grass. We built it to be private by default: the app has no user accounts, shows no ads, and does not track you. Most of your information stays on your device. This policy explains the few cases where data leaves your device, and why.

Information we handle

  • Lawn photos (only when you run an AI diagnosis). When you choose to scan a photo, that image is sent to our diagnostics service and to our AI provider (Anthropic) to generate the result. The photo is used only to produce your diagnosis, is not stored by us afterward, is not linked to your identity, and is not used to train AI models. If you never use the AI scan feature, no photos ever leave your device.
  • Location. If you grant location access (or set a region for a lawn), we use your location or region to fetch a local weather forecast (Apple WeatherKit, with Open-Meteo as a fallback) and to provide regional context for an AI diagnosis. We do not store your location on our servers or use it to track you.
  • Lawn-care data you enter — journal entries, soil tests, schedules, and watering logs, including any photos you attach to entries — is stored only on your device and is not transmitted to us.
  • Subscriptions. If you subscribe to Big Grass Guy Pro, the purchase is handled entirely by Apple through the App Store. We never receive your payment details; we only learn from Apple whether your subscription is active.

How we use information

We use the information above only to provide the features you ask for — diagnosing a photo, showing your local weather, and unlocking Pro. We do not use it for advertising, profiling, or tracking, and we do not sell or rent it to anyone.

Service providers

We share data only to the extent needed to run these features:

  • Anthropic — processes the photo you submit to generate the AI diagnosis.
  • Apple — provides WeatherKit forecasts and processes any subscription purchase.
  • Open-Meteo — a fallback weather provider when WeatherKit is unavailable.

These providers process the data on our behalf to deliver the feature and under their own terms.

Data retention

Photos submitted for diagnosis are processed in transit to produce your result and are not retained on our servers afterward. On-device data remains until you delete the relevant entries, lawn, or the app itself.

Your choices

  • You control camera, photo-library, and location permissions in iOS Settings, and can change them at any time.
  • You can delete any lawn, journal entry, soil test, or log within the app, and deleting the app removes all on-device data.
  • You can manage or cancel a subscription in your Apple ID account settings.

Children

Big Grass Guy is not directed to children and does not knowingly collect information from children.

Changes to this policy

If we change this policy, we will update the “Last updated” date above and post the new version at this URL.
